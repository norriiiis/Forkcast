# Forkcast — Visual Brain

> The complete brand system. Paste this whole document into a Claude Project as the
> instructions, and every chat in that project will produce on-brand Forkcast content
> automatically. Works in any AI tool. Update it as the brand evolves.

---

## Identity

Forkcast decides what's for dinner so people don't have to. It's the cheap, opinionated
meal planner that gives **one good answer instead of an endless library**. Set your
preferences once; every week it returns three things: a meal plan of 4–6 dinners whose
ingredients overlap, one aisle-sorted grocery list with the cost shown up front, and a
~90-minute Sunday prep schedule that turns weeknights into 10-minute assembly.

**Tagline: "Plan once. Eat all week."**

It decides, it doesn't suggest. It's software, not boxes — you shop your own store at
grocery prices (~$5/plate vs. ~$12 for meal kits). The brand is the friendly, capable
friend who already figured out dinner for you.

## Audience

One narrow persona: 24–38, works full-time, lives alone or with a partner. Wants to eat
better and spend less but has **low weeknight energy**. Already watches meal-prep content;
has tried meal prepping and fell off — because *planning* is the chore, not cooking.
They'll happily pay the price of one coffee a month to never plan dinner again.

They're scrolling tired. Content has to feel like relief, not another task.

## Voice Rules

- **Decisive and calm.** Short declarative sentences. "Dinner, decided." Never wishy-washy.
- **Warm and plain-spoken.** Talk like a friend, not a nutritionist or a brand.
- **Anti-diet-culture, always.** Easy, fast, cheap, good. **Never** "guilt-free," "clean,"
  "skinny," "cheat meal," "detox," "earn it," "sinful," or calorie/macro shaming.
- **Show the relief, not the hustle.** We remove a chore. We are not grind/optimization culture.
- **End on the artifact.** Every piece of content ends on the real thing — the plan, the
  list, the price. The product is the proof.
- **Specific over generic.** Real dinners, real dollar amounts, real overlap ("one pack of
  chicken → three meals"). Numbers earn trust.
- Light wit is welcome; sarcasm and shame are not.

## Colour System

### Palette

| Name | Hex | Role |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Oat** | `#FBF8F2` | Primary **light** background. Warm cream — appetizing, never clinical white. |
| **Forest** | `#14532D` | Primary **dark** background + deepest brand green. Hooks, pivots, CTAs, depth. |
| **Basil** | `#15803D` | Core brand green. Logo, emphasis words, buttons/CTAs on light, "savings" highlights. |
| **Ember** | `#EA580C` | The single highlight accent. Key numbers, "tap," one focal pop. Max **one** per slide. |
| **Char** | `#1C1917` | Primary text on light backgrounds. |
| **Stone** | `#6F6A64` | Secondary / supporting text on light. Eyebrows, captions, fine print. |
| **Sage** | `#E7EEE4` | Light surface tint — cards, panels, dividers on an Oat background. |

Text on **Forest** is **Oat** (`#FBF8F2`); supporting text on Forest is a muted sage `#A7C0AC`.

### Usage Rules

- **Backgrounds:** every surface is either **Oat** (light) or **Forest** (dark). Never pure
  white, never pure black, never a third background colour.
- **Emphasis:** make one or two words **Basil** (on light) to carry the point. On Forest,
  emphasis is **Ember** or kept in Oat with weight.
- **Ember is precious.** It appears at most once per slide, on the thing you want tapped or
  read first (a price, a CTA, the saved-dollars number). Overusing it kills it.
- **Savings/positive numbers → Basil. Hero price/CTA → Ember.** Keep this consistent.
- **Contrast:** body text is always **Char** on light / **Oat** on dark. Basil and Ember are
  for large or bold type only — never small body copy.

## Typography

Two Google Fonts. **Fraunces** (a warm, characterful display serif — editorial, appetizing,
confident) for headlines; **Inter** (clean, highly legible) for everything else.

### Hierarchy (sizes are for a 1080×1350 social canvas)

| Level | Font | Weight | Case | Size |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Hook headline** | Fraunces | 800–900 | Sentence case | 96–140 px, line-height 1.0–1.05 |
| **Headline / subhead** | Fraunces | 600–700 | Sentence case | 56–80 px |
| **Eyebrow / label** | Inter | 600 | UPPERCASE, +8% tracking | 24–28 px (Basil or Ember) |
| **Body** | Inter | 400–500 | Sentence case | 36–44 px, line-height 1.35 |
| **CTA text** | Inter | 700 | Sentence case | 40–52 px |
| **Big number** (price/savings) | Fraunces | 900 | — | 120–200 px |

### Text Rules

- **Hook ≤ 8 words. Body slide ≤ 15 words. Never more than ~25 words on any slide.**
- One idea per slide. If it needs two thoughts, it's two slides.
- Minimum text size 28 px so it's legible on a phone.
- Headlines sentence case with a period for that "decided" feel — *"Dinner, decided."* Avoid
  all-caps shouting (reserve uppercase for small eyebrows only).
- In the app/product, Fraunces for headings, Inter for UI and body; prices use Inter with
  tabular figures, weight 600.

## Imagery Direction

### Aesthetic

Warm, natural, genuinely appetizing **home food** — not glossy studio meal-kit ads. Real
dinners on real plates, generous portions, overhead flat-lays of finished meals and grocery
hauls, soft natural light, wood / linen / stoneware surfaces. It should look like a great
weeknight at home, photographed well. Bright but **warm**, never cool or clinical.

The single most important "image" is the **product artifact itself** — a screenshot of the
plan, the grocery list with its price, or the prep schedule. Content always ends on it.

### By Slide Position

- **Hook:** one strong food image (or a bold Forest colour slide) with the headline over a
  Forest scrim. High appetite appeal, stops the scroll.
- **Body / Value:** mostly clean Oat colour slides with type; one mid-slide shows a real
  dinner photo or the method.
- **Tension:** Forest slide, minimal — a comparison or a sharp line, little or no image.
- **CTA:** no food photo — a designed slide (Basil/Forest) with the logo lockup and tagline.

### Colour Treatment

Warm grade: nudge warmth up, keep saturation natural so food looks delicious, soft contrast,
a touch of film warmth is fine. **Avoid** cool/teal-orange Instagram filters, heavy
desaturation, heavy vignettes, and clinical bright-white.

### Text Placement

Text sits **top or bottom**, never across the food's focal point. Over an image, always lay a
**Forest gradient scrim** (transparent → Forest ~88% at the edge) so type stays readable.

### Off-Brand — never use

Corporate/business stock, staged "perfect" meal-kit boxes, neon palettes, cartoon mascots,
cool/clinical tones, and **anything diet-culture**: scales, measuring tape, before/after
bodies, calorie/macro overlays, green-juice "wellness" clichés.

## Layout and Composition

### Dimensions

- **Primary:** Instagram/TikTok portrait carousel **1080 × 1350** (4:5).
- **Stories / Reels cover:** 1080 × 1920.
- **Secondary:** square 1080 × 1080.

### Text Positioning

Hooks anchor **top-left or centered** with generous space below. Value slides put the
eyebrow + headline at the top and the supporting visual/line beneath. Left-aligned by default;
centered only on the CTA.

### Margins and Safe Zones

- **96 px** padding on all sides of a 1080-wide canvas (generous breathing room — warm, not dense).
- Stories: keep all content inside the top 250 px / bottom 320 px safe zone.

### Text on Image

Allowed only over a Forest scrim, bottom- or top-anchored, never on the focal point of the food.

### Density

Generous negative space. **One idea, max ~3 elements per slide** (eyebrow + headline + one
support). When in doubt, remove something.

## Visual Rhythm

### Background Pattern (7-slide carousel)

`S1 Forest · S2 Oat · S3 Oat · S4 Forest · S5 Oat · S6 Forest · S7 Basil`

Dark bookends and pivots; light, calm middle. Never two identical-feeling slides back to back.

### Image Pattern

Hero food image on **S1**. The real Forkcast artifact (plan / list / price) appears around
**S4–S5** as the payoff. CTA (**S7**) has no photo — it's designed.

### Energy Arc

Bold, high-contrast **hook** → calm, informative **middle** → sharp **tension** (the cost
comparison) → confident, warm **CTA**. Start loud, earn trust quietly, close with certainty.

## Content Architecture

**Standard carousel: 7 slides.**

| Slide | Role | Rules |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **1 — Hook** | Stop the scroll | ≤ 8 words, Fraunces 800+, Forest bg, food image + scrim |
| **2 — Context** | Name the pain | "~14 dinner decisions a week, all at 6pm." ≤ 15 words |
| **3–5 — Value** | The substance | The 3-part method: overlap → one list → 90-min prep; one slide shows the real artifact |
| **6 — Tension** | The pivot | Cost/contrast: "Meal kits fix this for ~$12 a plate. Forkcast does it for ~$5 — at your own store." |
| **7 — CTA** | The close | Tagline + handle + offer. Designed, never empty. |

- **Hook slide:** ≤ 8 words, the biggest type on the canvas, one Basil/Ember emphasis word.
- **Body slides:** ≤ 15 words, one idea, lots of space.
- **CTA slide:** Basil or Forest background, centered logo lockup, **"Plan once. Eat all
  week."**, the handle, and one Ember action line ("Your first week is free."). Must look
  *designed* — never a bare line of text.

## Brand Marks

- **Logo — the "fork-forecast" mark.** A fork whose four tines step **upward like a forecast
  chart** — the name is *fork + forecast*. Reads as both a fork and "things getting better."
  Rendered in **Basil** on light, **Oat** on dark. Files in `brand/`:
  `logo-mark.svg` (icon only), `logo-lockup.svg` (mark + wordmark), `app-icon.svg` (rounded tile).
- **Wordmark:** "Forkcast" in **Fraunces 800**, sentence case.
- **Handle:** `@forkcast` *(placeholder — confirm and secure the real handle across TikTok /
  Instagram / YouTube before launch).*
- **Position:** on content slides, the wordmark sits **bottom-left** and the handle
  **bottom-right**, both small and quiet. On the CTA slide, a centered logo lockup.
- **Sign-off / tagline:** **"Plan once. Eat all week."** — appears on every CTA slide and
  profile.
- **Consistent elements on every piece:** the fork-forecast mark, the Oat/Forest palette,
  Fraunces headlines, and ending on the real plan/list/price.

### How to use this

1. Paste this whole document into a **Claude Project** as the project instructions.
2. In that project, ask for content (e.g. *"Write a 7-slide carousel about why meal prep
   keeps failing"*) and it will follow every rule above.
3. When something comes back off — type too small, a slide too dense — adjust this document
   and re-paste. Every correction makes the system sharper.
